A multifunctional soft robotic shape display

(published Open Access in Nature Communications: https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-023-39842-2)

This work, part of my Ph.D. thesis, focuses on the development and control of a multifunctional, high-degree-of-freedom (DoF) shape display. This video gives an overview and highlights the capabilities of the device:

In this project, my coauthors and I developed the "soft" shape display you see above. The device is driven by an array of 100 "hydraulically-amplified self-healing electrostatic actuators" (HASEL actuators) which are both strong and energy efficient. We also incorporated an array of 100 Hall effect-based position sensors, one for each actuator, enabling both displacement- and force-feedback.

As a lead author of the project (together with my coauthor Mantas Naris), I oversaw the fabrication and testing of the shape display, characterized the actuator performance and implemented feedback control strategies (more on that below), and developed the variety of technical demonstrations you can see in the overview video. I also carried out the paper publication process including writing, editing, developing figure graphics and videos (like the one above), and working with Nature editorial staff to publish our work in Nature Communications based on peer reviewed feedback.
